If I could open a business in Saint Petersburg, I would start a modern Karelian bakery called “Kalitka” right on the Polytech university campus.

Kalitki are traditional pastries from Karelia, but they are not very popular among young people today. My idea is to make this traditional food **trendy** and interesting again.

In my bakery, customers could try both classic savory kalitki with potato or millet, and sweet ones with cranberry or blueberry. We would also offer new creative versions, including vegetarian and halal options. To complete the atmosphere, I would design a cozy interior reflecting Karelian culture.

Polytech is a modern and stylish university with thousands of students. This place would be perfect for those who want to try something folkish during their lunch break. It would also be a healthy alternative to the famous local pyshki, offering affordable and nutritious food.

I believe this business would be successful because it perfectly combines tradition and innovation. It would help preserve local culture while creating a modern, comfortable space for people to hang out. In conclusion, my Karelian bakery would not only be a profitable business, but also a delicious way to introduce people to Karelian heritage.
